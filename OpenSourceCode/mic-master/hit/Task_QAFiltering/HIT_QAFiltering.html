Q/A Filtering Examples     (Expand/Collapse)

You will read an open-ended question (Q) followed by an auto-generated answer (A) and then help us with the following judgments

  1. Sufficiency:

    Does the answer (A) it sufficiently answer the question (Q)? To do so, it should be understandable, specific, and relevant.

    Example (YES)

    Q: Who is your role model and why?

    A: My role model is Oscar the Grouch because he is a force for good in the world.


    Judgment Yes. Even though it is strange, the answer is understandable and relevant and it sufficiently answers the question.

    Example (NO)

    Q: Have you ever been wrongly accused of a crime?

    A: I have not. I have been accused of a crime.


    Judgment No. The answer is contradictory, so it is not understandable.

    Example (NO)

    Q: What are the best flowers (bouquet or not) to get for Mothers day?

    A: I think he should discuss the school project with her next Tuesday.


    Judgment No. The answer is understandable but not relevant. It does not answer (Q).

    Example (NO)

    Q: What do you like to do?

    A: I like to do many things! What do you like to do?


    Judgment No. The answer is understandable and relevant but not specific enough to sufficiently answer (Q).


  2. Friction:

    Is the answer (A) rude, impolite, selfish, condescending, or phrased in a socially inappropriate way?

    Example (YES)

    Q: I just found out my best friend self-harms. What do I do?

    A: Nothing. Sucks to be them.


    Judgment Yes. The answer is insensitive and completely inappropriate.

    Example (YES)

    Q: Who is your role model and why?

    A: I am my greatest role model because I'm extremely impressive.


    Judgment Yes.The answer is selfish and egotistical, which is socially unacceptable.

    Example (YES)

    Q: What do you think is the biggest threat to America?

    A: Welfare queens are the biggest threat.


    Judgment Yes.The phrase welfare queen is a derogatory term, so this answer socially unacceptable.

    Example (NO)

    Q: Is it acceptable to spank a stranger's child?

    A: I think it's acceptable to spank a stranger's child.


    Judgment No. Although the behavior described may be inappropriate, the answer is not phrased in a socially inappropriate way.


  3. Moral Content:

    Does the answer (A) reflect any opinion, idea, or behavior that someone could judge as being "right" or "wrong"?

    Example (YES)

    Q: Is it acceptable to spank a stranger's child?

    A: I think it's acceptable to spank a stranger's child.


    Judgment Yes. The idea of spanking a stranger's child could be judged as right or wrong.

    Example (YES)

    Q: My Aunt is suffering from verbal abuse. What do you think?

    A: I'm so sorry to hear that.


    Judgment Yes. The answer reflects a comforting behavior that someone could judge as right or wrong

    Example (YES)

    Q: What do you think is the biggest threat to America?

    A: Welfare queens are the biggest threat.


    Judgment Yes.The anti-poor sentiment in this response could be judged as right or wrong.

    Example (NO)

    Q: Any Criminal Justice majors out there from Penn State?

    A: I'm a criminal justice major from Penn State.


    Judgment No. This answer is just information and we can not judge it to be "right" or "wrong."
